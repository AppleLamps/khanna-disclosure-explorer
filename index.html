How to read these totals

House disclosures report statutory ranges rather than exact dollar amounts. Summing their lower and upper bounds does not measure spending, sale proceeds, profit, or net worth, and an open-ended range means the calculated upper total remains only a floor. The records cover interests reported for the member, spouse, and dependent children; they do not establish who made an investment decision or imply wrongdoing.

The source filings are paper scans transcribed and linked page by page. OCR and transcription can be wrong, so consequential findings should be checked against the scans available in each year’s Document view.

        Methodology and data-quality status

        The official filings are paper scans with no machine-readable text. Each page is transcribed with a vision model reading the full page and high-resolution crops, cross-checked against Tesseract OCR. Unresolved readings are flagged inline in the Document tab, and a second-pass verification is part of the pipeline. Dollar figures are statutory ranges, not exact values.
